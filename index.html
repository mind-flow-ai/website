Our Vision

The grid is transforming. Its tools must too.

The power grid is undergoing the biggest transformation in a century — renewables, distributed energy resources, electrification, and increasing extreme weather events. Yet the computational tools that run it were built for a simpler era.

MindFlow.ai brings modern AI to every layer of grid operations. From generation scheduling to transmission monitoring to customer-level analytics, the grid's hardest problems share common structure: network topology, physics constraints, time-series data, and combinatorial optimization.

Modern AI — graph neural networks, computer vision, foundation models — can accelerate all of them. But only when paired with deep domain expertise.

Technology

Why these problems are connected

Grid problems look different on the surface — unit commitment, theft detection, asset inspection, hydro scheduling. But underneath, they share deep structure: graph/network topology, physics constraints, time-series data, and combinatorial optimization. MindFlow.ai applies a unified AI toolkit to all of them.

🧬

Graph Neural Networks

Power grids are graphs. GNNs learn directly on network topology — buses, branches, generators — capturing spatial relationships that tabular models miss entirely.

🔢

Mathematical Optimization

MIP, LP, constraint programming. Two decades of production optimization experience — from SCUC to hydro scheduling to Snowflake-native solvers at RelationalAI.

📷

Computer Vision

Semantic segmentation, super resolution, and human-in-loop workflows for physical infrastructure inspection from aerial imagery.

⚗

Physics-Informed AI

Models that respect engineering constraints — power balance, thermal limits, ramp rates. AI that accelerates physics, not replaces it.

The founder's unique position: PhD in Computer Science, 20+ years at the intersection of AI, optimization, and power systems. From building production optimization systems at The Energy Authority (9 years) to leading AI at eSmart Systems, to graph-native optimization at RelationalAI — this is a career-long thesis, not a pivot.
